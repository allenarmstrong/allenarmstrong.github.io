Introduction

08 Feb 2015

Hello and welcome. I've decided to redesign my main site allenarmstrong.com into 2 identities — My Music and this site, The Words I Write. The old site was a portfolio site to show off my work to clients or potenial new employers, but as I tell recruiters (almost everday)... I'm not looking for work right now. I'm very happy with where I'm at now. I'm a web desinger/developer for Florida Virtual School and I really am happy and love what I'm doing. I design the course-ware that educates the youth of Florida and wordwide. I've been designing and building websites since the mid 90's, and most of them have been in one way or the other, trying to sell you something. Cars, Motorcycles, Vacations, Software, Video games, clothing, stocks... you name it, I've built a site trying to sell it. What I'm doing at FLVS is so rewarding, knowing it's educating the youth. I get to work from home, I get paid well and the people are the greatest... whats not to love, why would I want a new job?

The previous site was a Wordpress site... and like so many others, I decided that it's become a little more than what I really need. I've used it since 07 or 08 when I first started blogging. I had a photoblog - I Don't Blame You - for awhile and many other little projects and sites running on WP. But now I just want something that would be easy to maintain, fast with little overhead... don't have to worry about constantly applying security patches and updates. I've been reading about flat-file CMS's for the last year or so, and even played around a little with some. I've finally decided to go in on Jekyll.

So what is this site going to be about... basically a little about everything. I'll probably still post about projects at work, or some of my photography... so it will still sorta be a portfolio... but in a different format. A personal portfolio.

But this just be a brain dump - link sharing blog. I didn't want it to be. And I think that's why I decided to go with a flat-file system as well.. thats one of the beauties of it. I think it will require more thought and purpose behind every post. To go through and format your post, the republish, commit the changes, ect. It's more methodical, more to the process... and I like that. It's almost like an art, each step important in the total outcome. Like back in the day, when I wrote more often for zines - it was a process and an art.
